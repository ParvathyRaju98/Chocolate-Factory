Chocolate Unwrapped

An analytic guide to your healthiest chocolate bar

Introduction to project

This project is an attempt to conduct a comparitive study to understand which Chocolate Bar is the healthiest option out there in the Market. In todays world of health and diet consciousness we tend to always read labels but alas fall for the bright packaging and false advertising. This visualisaton entails to break down the parameters of what really defines a healthy chocolate bar and tends to study market patterns to help the consumer in the same

So how do you find the healthiest chocolate bar for you?

Know your
ingredients

An average chocolate bar contains all or
some of these ingredients in them

Calories

The amount of energy in an item of food or drink is measured in calories. When we eat and drink more calories than we use up, our body stores the excess as body fat


Carbohydrates

They are sugar molecules. Along with protein and fats, carbohydrates are one of the three main nutrients found in food and drinks. Your body breakdowns carbs into glucose. Glucose or blood sugar is the main source of energy for your body cell, tissues and organs


Saturated Fats

This is a type of dietary fat. It is one of the unhealthy fats along with trans fat. These fats are mostly solid at room temperature. Food like butter, palm and coconut oils, cheese and red meat have high amounts of unsaturated fats.


Trans Fats

Also called trans-unsaturated fatty acids or trans fatty acids, is a type of unsaturated fat that naturally occurs in small amounts in meat and milky fat. Trans fat is considered the worst type of fat to consume. Unlike other dietary fats, they raise “bad” cholesterol and also lower good cholesterol. A diet laden with trans fat increases the risk of heart disease.


Proteins

These are made up of chemical building blocks called amino acids. Your body uses amino acids to build and repair muscles and bones and to make hormones and enzymes. They can also be used as an energy source.


Sodium

Sodium is found naturally in foods, but a lot of it is added during processing and preparation. Many foods that do not taste salty maybe still high in sodium. A large amount of sodium can be found in canned, processed or convenience foods.


Cholesterol

Cholesterol is fat like waxy substance that helps your body make cell membranes, many hormones, and Vitamin D. Cholesterol in your blood comes from two sources, the food you eat and your liver. Saturated fats such as those in meat, butter, cheese and other full-fat dairy products raise your total cholesterol.
